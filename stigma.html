Stigma vs Truth

Whilst a symptom of these disorders can be unpredictable behaviour, most people with these disorders are not violent - especially those who are going through treatment.
Disorders have many underlying causes including genetics, drug abuse and trauma from past experiences. Making mistakes when raising a child will not directly result in them having a disorder.
However hopeless the situation may seem in the moment, the smallest things can make a big difference to people struggling with their mental health. Talking to them, encouraging healthy coping mechanisms and spending time with the person struggling can help remind them that they are supported when times get tough.
Mental illnesses are treatable regardless of age, race, gender or class. When treated, people with mental health problems can live very successful and fulfiling lives.
While it may sound like a positive approach, not all mental health problems can be recovered quicker through effort.

All mental health problems are different, and placing strain on yourself to recover risks putting yourself into further mental health problems and stress.

If you wish to get treatment for your mental health please seek a professional's advice to reduce the risk of overworking yourself and going down the wrong road to recovery.

If you need someone to talk to about your mental health or need support in your current situation, our Resources page has a list of services available for you whenever, wherever.
