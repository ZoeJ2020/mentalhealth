Coping Mechanisms

Coping mechanisms can help reduce stress and regulate emotions during difficult situations. However, some coping mechanisms such as overeating or losing sleep can be dangerous to your mental wellbeing.

Below are some healthy coping mechanisms to keep in mind when feeling overwhelmed.

Clean around the house

Taking a few minutes out of the day to clean your room or other places in the house can give your brain a moment to slow down. Keeping your house tidy also helps you feel less stressed compared to a constantly messy living space.

Keep a well-balanced diet

Your body and brain work best when fuelled with the right nutrients. Eating too much junk food that has high levels of salt and fat can leave you feeling more tired and lethargic than if you had eaten home-cooked nutritious food.

Take a walk outside

Exercise can serve as a natural and healthy form of stress relief. Running, yoga, swimming, walking, dance, team sports, and many other types of physical activity can help people cope with stress and the aftereffects of traumatic events.

Talk about your concerns

If difficulties in your life are getting to you, reach out to someone you trust to try and help. This can be a family member, friend, coworker or a teacher. Having someone know what you're going through can bring relief in knowing you're not struggling on your own.

If you need someone to talk to but don't know where to go, our Resources page gives a list of helplines that are available to contact.
