Resources

In an emergency?

If you or someone you know has seriously harmed themselves please call 999 for an ambulance or go to the nearest A&E for emergency treatment.

Sometimes you need someone to talk to when nobody else will listen. These organisations prioritise your needs and are ready when you need them most.

Samaritans

Samaritans are available to anybody struggling with mental health that needs support.
They are open 24/7, 365 days a year.

Website: https://www.samaritans.org/
Phone: 116 123 from any device, 24/7
Email: jo@samaritans.org
In person: Find branches near you

Childline

Childline offers support to any child or young person (up to 19 years old) in the UK that is concerned about their mental health.

Website: https://www.childline.org.uk/
Phone: 0800 1111 from any device 9am - 3.30am
Email: https://www.childline.org.uk/locker/inbox (with a Childline account)

SANE

SANE is a mental health charity dedicated to improving the lives of people with poor mental health.

Website: www.sane.org.uk
Phone: 07984 967 708 - Leave a name and contact number
Email: support@sane.org.uk

OCD UK

OCD UK is a charity run by people with OCD to support others with obsessive compulsive disorders.
Their content includes information about OCD, news and different forms of treatment.

Website: www.ocduk.org
Phone: 0333 212 7890 - Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm
Email: www.ocduk.org/contact-us/

SHOUT

SHOUT is a free text service for people who need someone to talk to about their concerns.
The service is completely anonymous, free and available 24/7.

Website: www.giveusashout.org
Phone: Text SHOUT to 85258, 24/7
Email: info@giveusashout.org - Please note that this is for questions regarding the service.
This email is not for mental health support.
